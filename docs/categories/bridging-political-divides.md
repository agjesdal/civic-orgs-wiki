# Bridging Political Divides

Organizations whose primary work is reducing political polarization and rebuilding the capacity of people across the partisan spectrum to understand one another. Approaches range from facilitated workshops and structured conversations to large-scale polarization research and field-wide coalitions.

## Organizations

- [Braver Angels](../organizations/braver-angels.md) — Cross-partisan, volunteer-led citizens’ movement that unites red and blue Americans through workshops, debates, and local alliances to depolarize America.
- [Constructive Dialogue Institute](../organizations/constructive-dialogue-institute.md) — Psychology-based nonprofit (formerly OpenMind) building educational tools — including the Perspectives blended learning program — that equip students, faculty, and workplaces with the mindset and skills for cross-difference dialogue.
- [Listen First Project](../organizations/listen-first-project.md) — Backbone organization for the #ListenFirst Coalition (~500 bridging organizations) and the Bridging Movement Alignment Council; aggregates and amplifies depolarization efforts and runs National Week of Conversation.
- [Living Room Conversations](../organizations/living-room-conversations.md) — Free, open-source conversation guides that help small groups across political, generational, racial, and other divides have structured one-hour conversations using shared agreements.
- [More in Common](../organizations/more-in-common.md) — International research nonprofit (US, UK, France, Germany, Poland, Spain, Brazil) that studies the psychology of polarization and “perception gaps,” then partners with civil society, media, faith, and business actors to counter “us vs.
- [Sustained Dialogue Institute](../organizations/sustained-dialogue-institute.md) — Nonprofit applying the Sustained Dialogue peace process (developed by diplomat Harold Saunders) through a campus network, workplaces, and communities; defines dialogue as “listening deeply enough to be changed by what you learn.”
