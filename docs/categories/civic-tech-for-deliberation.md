# Civic Tech for Deliberation & Participation

Organizations building tools, platforms, and processes that let groups deliberate, surface consensus, and participate in collective decisions at scale. Includes both software platforms and the practitioner networks and research bodies that support deliberative democracy.

## Organizations

- [Collective Intelligence Project (CIP)](../organizations/collective-intelligence-project.md) — 501(c)(3) building tools and methods for democratic oversight of AI; runs “Alignment Assemblies” (often via Polis) that surface public input on AI behavior in partnership with frontier labs and governments.
- [Common (One Project)](../organizations/common-one-project.md) — Nonprofit building Common, an open platform for organizations and municipalities to collectively allocate resources — participatory budgeting and democratic decision-making at scale.
- [Democracy Developers (Ask Parliament)](../organizations/democracy-developers.md) — Australian nonprofit building open-source software for democracy; Ask Parliament lets citizens crowdsource questions for MPs and parliamentary committees.
- [Democracy R&D](../organizations/democracy-rd.md) — International network of ~95 organizations advancing deliberative democracy — primarily citizens’ assemblies and sortition-based processes — through annual convenings and peer learning.
- [Metagov](../organizations/metagov.md) — Nonprofit interdisciplinary research collective building standards and infrastructure for digital self-governance; ran a Mina Foundation–funded cohort that brought 21 deliberative-tool teams to minimum viable interoperability.
- [newDemocracy Foundation](../organizations/newdemocracy-foundation.md) — Australian nonpartisan research and development organization that designs and runs citizens’ juries and other deliberative mini-publics; co-founder of the Democracy R&D network.
- [Plurality Institute](../organizations/plurality-institute.md) — Research hub for “plurality” — technology for human cooperation at scale — convening academics, technologists, and civic leaders; builds tools including BridgingBot, an LLM-powered moderator that fosters constructive online dialogue.
- [Polis](../organizations/polis.md) — Open-source, AI-assisted conversation platform (stewarded by the nonprofit Computational Democracy Project) that surfaces areas of consensus and division in large-group deliberation; used at national scale in Taiwan.
- [Staked Voice](../organizations/staked-voice.md) — Pre-product Swiss-hosted platform where verified humans privately rank civic priorities and directional leanings; “count and forget” architecture with inverted terms of service.
