# Civic & Democracy Organizations Wiki

A navigable reference of organizations working in the civic and democracy space — bridging political divides, deliberative civic technology, open inquiry, healthy information ecosystems, and related fields.

Browse by category below. Each category page lists its organizations; each organization has its own page with mission and notes. Organizations may appear in more than one category.

## Categories

- **[Bridging Political Divides](categories/bridging-political-divides.md)** (6) — Organizations whose primary work is reducing political polarization and rebuilding the capacity of people across the partisan spectrum to understand one another. Approaches range from facilitated workshops and structured conversations to large-scale polarization research and field-wide coalitions.
- **[Civic Tech for Deliberation & Participation](categories/civic-tech-for-deliberation.md)** (9) — Organizations building tools, platforms, and processes that let groups deliberate, surface consensus, and participate in collective decisions at scale. Includes both software platforms and the practitioner networks and research bodies that support deliberative democracy.
- **[Open Inquiry in Academia](categories/open-inquiry-in-academia.md)** (4) — Organizations focused on viewpoint diversity, academic freedom, free expression, and constructive disagreement within higher education and research institutions.
- **[Digital Sovereignty & Data Governance](categories/digital-sovereignty-and-data-governance.md)** (4) — Organizations concerned with who controls technology, data, and AI — building infrastructure, standards, and governance models that give people and the public greater agency in the digital economy.
- **[Information Ecosystem & Media Transparency](categories/information-ecosystem-and-media-transparency.md)** (4) — Organizations working on the health of the information environment — media bias transparency, balanced news, healthier digital public spaces, and evidence-based approaches to online discourse.
- **[Tech & Social Cohesion (Prosocial Design)](categories/tech-and-social-cohesion.md)** (4) — Organizations working to shift technology design toward social cohesion, trust, and constructive interaction — the emerging field of prosocial design and tech-for-cohesion.
- **[Regenerative & Systems-Change Civic Movements](categories/regenerative-systems-change-movements.md)** (1) — Organizations oriented around systemic transformation, regenerative approaches, and civic innovation that works at the level of whole systems rather than single issues.
- **[International Justice & Human Rights](categories/international-justice-and-human-rights.md)** (1) — Organizations whose mission centers on international human rights enforcement, accountability, and justice mechanisms.
- **[Outlier (Non-Democracy)](categories/outlier-non-democracy.md)** (1) — Organizations included in earlier research passes whose mission does not actually fall within the civic or democracy space. Retained here for completeness and review.
